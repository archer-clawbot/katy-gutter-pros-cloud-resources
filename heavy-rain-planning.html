Roof edge to discharge

Seven heavy-rain gutter planning checks

Width is one input. Performance depends on whether water enters the gutter, reaches adequate outlets, moves through matched downspouts, and exits at an appropriate endpoint.

Role clarity: Katy Gutter Pros publishes independent information. It does not design or install gutter systems.

01

Roof catchment

Measure projected roof area feeding each run and divide the roof by drainage direction.

02

Pitch and valleys

Identify where flow concentrates and where water overshoots or overwhelms a short section.

03

Profile, size, and slope

Ask what measurements and sizing method support the recommendation. A larger gutter cannot correct a blocked outlet.

04

Outlets

Record quantity, dimensions, and placement. Check for debris bridging the transition.

05

Downspouts

Match leaders to outlets and note elbows, offsets, dents, separated sections, and buried connections.

06

Debris and guards

Match protection to debris and roof conditions while preserving inspection and cleaning access.

07

Discharge

Trace every endpoint and note ponding, erosion, walkways, boundaries, grade, and specialist needs.

Questions for a measured estimate

  1. What roof area feeds each run?
  2. Where is flow concentrated?
  3. Why was this profile and size selected?
  4. How many outlets are proposed, and where?
  5. Are downspouts matched to the outlets?
  6. Where does every downspout discharge?
  7. Which conditions are excluded?
