Property-input checklist

Prepare for a measured gutter scope in Katy

A useful proposal describes one connected path: roof catchment, gutter run, outlets, downspouts, and final discharge.

Publisher disclosure: Katy Gutter Pros is an independent gutter-information and contractor-matching publisher, not a gutter contractor.

1. Record the visible symptom

Note no or incomplete coverage, leaking joints, sagging, overflow, standing water, repeated debris, damaged downspouts, or discharge that collects in a problem area. Use safe ground-level photos; do not climb during rain.

2. Map the roof sections

Ask the estimator to identify projected roof area, pitch, valleys, run lengths, corners, fascia and roof-edge conditions, and practical outlet locations. Interior floor area does not determine gutter footage or roof catchment.

3. Follow water through the outlets

Record the number, dimensions, and placement of outlets. Overflow near one opening can indicate a restriction, tight elbow, or backed-up discharge route rather than an automatic need for a wider gutter.

4. Document each downspout and endpoint

List downspout dimensions, elbows, offsets, straps, transitions, extensions, and endpoints. Note patios, walks, landscaping, neighboring property, and low areas that constrain discharge.

5. Turn labels into specifications

Require profile, nominal size, material, thickness or gauge, finish, hanger type and spacing, fasteners, outlet and downspout dimensions, sealants, connection points, guard product, and written warranty terms. “Seamless” does not remove joints at corners, end caps, outlets, and transitions.

6. Define inclusions and exclusions

Clarify removal, disposal, upper-story access, fascia or soffit work, roof-edge corrections, underground drainage, permits, HOA coordination, cleanup, taxes, and change orders.

7. Compare equivalent scopes

Do not compare totals until measurements and specifications align. Use the estimate comparison worksheet and the Katy Gutter Pros cost guide.

Ten questions to take to an appointment

  1. What measured roof area feeds each run?
  2. Why was this profile and size selected?
  3. Where are the outlets, and why?
  4. Are downspouts matched to the outlets?
  5. Where does every downspout discharge?
  6. Which conditions are excluded?
  7. Who handles related roof-edge, grading, or underground work?
  8. Which written warranties apply?
  9. What can trigger a change order?
  10. What evidence should be reviewed before hiring?
